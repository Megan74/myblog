## Debgihjgh-ing ❌ .. debugh-ing❌ .. debugging ✅

 I always thought that being a web developer was all about the buidling, creating, with a collaborative team. As matter of fact, I was partly wrong. As you advance in that career, in that job position path,
 you will  realize quickly enough, that most part of your missions is about correcting your code on the slightest, meaningless, forgetful mistakes: a whole code can be disrupted by a missing comma in your promise object using ajax syntax,
 a missing semi-colon can paralyze your whole CSS file. A misplaced capital letter to one of your variable or function exported to another React component, can cause you endless headaches.

 These bugs pushed me at first, to the apotheose of frustration, I will not lie, but then after a few deep breaths, relentless perserverence , my solving problem obession and unstoppable, I used and abused the google researches that led me eventually to overstackflow and github,
  to get the answers that I needed, to troubleshoot the bugs. And at one point I could **FINALLY** understood the pattern of every files, dependencies and package versions compatibilities intertwned with other, and the source the code disruption.

  Also, those code resolutions made me realize how **organized**, thorough, attentive, vigorous you have to be, to become an efficient and successful developer.

  The feeling of pride you get after spending hours, days, looking through your components, your server-side codebase, node modules, to one day finding why your console keeps
  displaying an error message, is **IMMEASURABLE**.

  That is also a new mantra that I managed to develop, ever since I started my journey in Tech: to NEVER give up, even when I feel like there is no solving's way out. Never give up, even when going around your codes for hours would make you feel that you are failing,
 it is all about keep perserving until it starts to make sense! Breaks work as well, that help to go to bed to recover after a good night of sleep.

My best friend are google stackoverflow & github, they are my everyday helping and learning tools❤️. They are my web developing compass.

## Trough of sorrow

To literally create this blog, I had to go through what it is called **THEE through of sorrow**.

Weeks, days, hours of debugging uncompatible modules, converting the right webpack into the right version for Node. 
Downloading the right package to make sure the mdx extensions will be parsed and uploade perfectly. Tons of sweat and sleepless nights,
googling how to debug every step of the building phase. I can tell you if you a re not a fighting soldier, that battle is lost in advance, building actual projects
are not for the weak. But at least, it is important to give self some slacks, also applaud self to the effort, time, motivation to open VS Code and decide to build something, 
having these pride moments when everything work smoothly (for few hours until... ❌ ), take breaks, go outside, socialize and come back recharged to the pended challenges.

It is okay also to give up one projects when the mountain is to hard and to high to climb, start with a simple project that is way more attainable and easier to develop, then push it to github, portfolio,
twitter, linkeding, so your web dev peers can I have an eye on it.

Sadly for me, my ambitious personality and untreated impatience to become the best web developer, pushe me to chose the most complicated projects ever created, just for the thrill and to 
prove myself how capable and how fast I learn. I can say that strategy is undoubtedly risky and proved to be exhausting, especially when facing very technical issues, that seem impossible to solve.

But again I NEVER give up, I will google, stackoverflow, github my way out of these unbuggable bugs, until I will make it work again.

Just to give you an idea, lately, I would consider my Atlas moment as my blog deplyment blog to vercel and to upload it online. These are my late list of debugs I getting myself into.
